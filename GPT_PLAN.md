## 1. Understanding the Project & My Role

WarSignal AirAssault is a **top-down AC-130 fantasy** with a cyberpunk soul. Mechanically, the player never leaves the orbit. Emotionally, the player never leaves the ground team.

My role as Narrative Designer is not to write lore dumps or cinematic monologues — it’s to **turn mechanical pressure into emotional pressure**. The ground team isn’t just an HP bar; they’re voices in your ear. When they panic, you panic. When they trust you, you feel responsible.

This game’s competitive advantage over Goliath isn’t graphics or weapons — it’s **human stakes**:

* Named operators
* Consistent radio chatter
* Escalating threat intelligence
* A villain that talks back

Narrative here is a **gameplay amplifier**, not a separate system.

## 3. Narrative Approach & Themes

### Core Themes

* **Dependence**: The ground team cannot win without you.
* **Distance**: You’re powerful, but never physically present.
* **Escalation**: Street thugs → PMCs → AI-directed warfare.
* **Trust**: Radio chatter builds reliance over time.
* **Responsibility**: Friendly fire isn’t just a fail state — it’s guilt.

### Structural Philosophy

* Every level answers one question and raises another.
* Radio chatter shifts from tactical → emotional → desperate.
* Antagonists *comment on player behavior* (speed, precision, brutality).

The Wraith never speaks — but the world responds to how you fight.

## 6. Risks & Concerns

1. **Overwriting during combat**
   → Radio chatter must be short, interruptible, and priority-based.

2. **Narrative fatigue**
   → Silence is a tool. Not every moment needs dialogue.

3. **Tone drift**
   → Cyberpunk, not camp. Humor stays dry and defensive.

4. **Player performance mismatch**
   → Avoid shaming language; react to outcomes, not skill.
